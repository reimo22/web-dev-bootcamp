My Programming Journey

Since I was young I had a penchant for puzzles and challenges which naturally led me to math and programming. I am a tinkerer, I rooted my first phone when I was 13 and experimented with custom ROMs and kernels, even bricking a phone in the process. (I used to run Resurrection Remix on my S5). I also regularly installed modded apks and used lucky patcher and game guardian on my games. I even had 99999 gems on Pixel Gun 3d at one point. There's also a time where I bought capacitors and tried (and failed) to build a homemade taser. I am generally the guy you call to get the TV, printer or wifi working or even just to assemble desks and cabinets. Did I say I also like lego?

Throughout grade school and high school we had some really good exposure to computing. We were taught how to use Word, PowerPoint and Excel to create documents and presentations. I had my first exposure to programming using Scratch, I remember my final project was a game show where you are asked to perform arithmetic from randomly generated numbers. Then we moved on to making games in Greenfoot, then animation in flash. We also learned some web development, and a little photo editing. Then I graduated from senior high.

That summer, I took an online course—CS50x where I learned to code in C, how to use google and stackoverflow and read documentation, as well as problem solving in general. Then I enrolled in computer science. In my first semester of college, we went over building static webpages using HTML5 and CSS3 and an introduction to programming in C#, which at this point is just a recap for me. Right now I'm studying webpage layout / design and javascript and taking CSS NC2. My current pastimes are reading webnovels or playing on codewars or hackerrank.
